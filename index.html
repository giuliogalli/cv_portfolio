📄Libertà
XYZ - Laboratorio di Ricerca e Co-progettazione, La Scuola Open Source
July 2018
An open source and generative typeface designed during the last edition of XYZ, a cooperative design workshop held in Bari (Italy) by La Scuola Open Source, as the corporate font for Officina degli Esordi, one of the most important cultural containers of southern Italy. All the typefaces are generated by a special notational and programming system. First, a text file is designed to depict the glyph, then, a python script scan the file and applies basic shapes (randomly) to it.
Credits & Resources:
-Roman design by me, Micol Salomone, Greta Capozzi, Roberto Lenza, Laura Laricchiuta
-Italic design by me
-Python script by Daniele Capo, Giovanni Abbatepaolo
More information about this project: http://www.lascuolaopensource.xyz/en/blog/xyz2018-output-finali https://github.com/lascuolaopensource/generatore-tipografico-di-liberta

📄Capraia 1.0
Self Initiated
February 2019—ongoing
A typeface I designed in the past months after I was on holiday with my girlfriend in Capraia (Italy), an island in the tuscanian archipelago. While we were hiking on the inland I found a stone with a “1938” carved upon it, I was fascinated by the 3, in particular, and I decided to use it to build Capraia.

📄ECTOPLAST
BA project, Isia Urbino
December 2017—June 2017
As students of the third year of the bachelor in graphic design at Isia Urbino, we moved for one week to Asolo (a small town in the north of Italy, in the Veneto region) where we were asked to investigate the particular status of the town, which is strongly depopulated but strives to maintain the narration of itself around famous persons who dealt with the town but don’t represent it anymore. Me and my team used an open source 3D modeling software to create “ghost-like” portraits of these famous persons and collected them in a book. One on these faces was 3D printed and exhibited next to the book on the end-of-the-year school exhibition.
